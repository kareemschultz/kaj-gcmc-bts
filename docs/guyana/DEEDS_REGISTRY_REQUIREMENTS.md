# Deeds and Commercial Registries Authority (DCRA) - Compliance Requirements

## Overview

The Deeds and Commercial Registries Authority (DCRA) is the government body responsible for registering and maintaining records of businesses, companies, property deeds, patents, trademarks, and other commercial transactions in Guyana.

**Official Website:** [dcra.gov.gy](https://dcra.gov.gy)
**Alternative Portal:** [dcraguyana.org](https://dcraguyana.org)

---

## Organizational Structure

The DCRA divides its functions into two main categories:

### 1. Deeds Registry Functions
- Registration of land titles and property deeds
- Transfer of property ownership
- Mortgages and liens
- Deeds of gift
- Leases

### 2. Commercial Registry Functions
- Incorporation of companies
- Registration of business names
- Registration of patents and designs
- Registration of bills of sales
- Execution of debentures
- External company registration
- Annual return processing
- Beneficial ownership declarations

---

## PART 1: BUSINESS NAME REGISTRATION

### Who Can Apply
Any individual **above the age of 18** may apply for a business name registration

### Processing Time
**3 working days** per application

### Application Process

1. **Name Search**
   - Check name availability (prevent duplicates)
   - Submit name reservation request
   - Receive approval/rejection

2. **Submit Application**
   - Complete business name registration form
   - Provide required documents
   - Pay application fee

3. **Receive Certificate**
   - Certificate of Business Registration issued
   - Valid for operation in Guyana

### Fees (Guyana Dollars)

| Service | Fee (GYD) |
|---------|----------|
| **Processing Fee** | $6,000 |
| **Renewal Fee** | $2,500 |
| **Statement of Change** | $2,000 |

### Required Documents
- Completed application form
- Valid identification (passport, national ID, driver's license)
- Proof of business address
- Name reservation approval (if applicable)

### Validity Period
Business name registrations require periodic renewal - check with DCRA for specific renewal timelines

## PART 2: COMPANY INCORPORATION

### Types of Companies

**Private Companies (Limited by Shares):**
- Most common business structure
- Limited liability protection
- Minimum 1 shareholder, maximum depends on company type
- Must have directors and company secretary

**Public Companies:**
- Can offer shares to the public
- More stringent reporting requirements
- Must comply with securities regulations

**Companies Limited by Guarantee:**
- Typically for non-profit organizations
- No share capital
- Members guarantee a specified amount

### Incorporation Process

#### Step 1: Name Reservation
- Conduct name search to ensure uniqueness
- Submit proposed company name
- Receive name reservation (valid for limited period)

#### Step 2: Prepare Incorporation Documents

**Required Documents:**

1. **Memorandum of Association**
   - Company name and registered office
   - Objects and purposes of the company
   - Share capital structure
   - Initial shareholders

2. **Articles of Association**
   - Internal governance rules
   - Director appointment procedures
   - Shareholder rights and obligations
   - Meeting procedures
   - Dividend policies

3. **Declaration of Compliance**
   - Sworn statement that all legal requirements met
   - Signed by director, company secretary, or attorney

4. **Notice of Registered Office**
   - Physical address in Guyana
   - Cannot be P.O. Box

5. **Particulars of Directors and Secretary**
   - Full names, addresses, occupations
   - Date of appointment
   - Consent to act (signatures required)

6. **Statement of Share Allocation**
   - Details of initial share distribution
   - Names and addresses of shareholders
   - Number and class of shares allocated

#### Step 3: Submit to DCRA
- File all documents with Commercial Registry
- Pay incorporation fees
- Await processing and review

#### Step 4: Certificate of Incorporation
- Issued upon approval
- Company officially exists as legal entity
- Can commence business operations

### Incorporation Fees
Contact DCRA for current incorporation fee schedule (varies by company type and share capital)

### Processing Time
Typically **1-2 weeks** depending on document completeness

## PART 3: ANNUAL RETURN FILING

### Who Must File
**All incorporated companies** must file annual returns, including:
- Private limited companies
- Public limited companies
- External companies operating in Guyana

### Filing Deadline
Annual returns are due within a specified period after the company's financial year-end (verify specific deadline with DCRA)

**Common Practice:** Many companies have December 31 year-end, making returns due by a certain date the following year

### What to Submit

**Annual Return Filing Package:**

1. **Completed Annual Return Form**
   - Company details
   - Registered office address
   - Directors and secretary information
   - Share capital and shareholders

2. **Financial Statements**
   - Balance sheet
   - Income statement (Profit & Loss)
   - Cash flow statement
   - Notes to financial statements
   - Auditor's report (if required)

3. **Updated Information**
   - Any changes to directors or secretary
   - Changes to registered office
   - Changes to share structure

### Importance of Annual Returns
- Maintains company in good standing
- Required for compliance certificates
- Failure to file can result in:
  - Penalties and fines
  - Company strike-off from register
  - Inability to transact certain business
  - Difficulty obtaining loans or contracts

## PART 4: BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP INFORMATION (BOI)

### Overview
The AML (Anti-Money Laundering) Department within DCRA is responsible for capturing Declaration of Beneficial Ownership information.

### Who Must File
All companies incorporated in Guyana must declare their beneficial owners

### What is a Beneficial Owner?
An individual who:
- Owns or controls (directly or indirectly) more than 25% of shares
- Exercises significant control over the company
- Has right to appoint/remove majority of directors
- Otherwise exercises ultimate ownership or control

### Filing Requirement
- Submit Beneficial Ownership Declaration
- Update when changes occur
- Maintain accurate and current information

### Penalties for Non-Submission
- Notice of Default issued
- Fines and penalties
- Potential company suspension or strike-off

---

## PART 5: CHANGES AND UPDATES

### When to Notify DCRA

Companies must notify DCRA of changes including:

**Director/Secretary Changes:**
- Appointment of new director
- Resignation of director
- Change of director details
- Appointment/resignation of company secretary

**Registered Office Change:**
- Moving to new business address
- Must maintain physical address in Guyana

**Share Structure Changes:**
- Allotment of new shares
- Transfer of shares
- Change in share classes

**Company Name Change:**
- Special resolution required
- Approval from DCRA
- Updated certificate issued

### Required Form
**Statement of Change** - Fee: $2,000 GYD

### Filing Deadline
Typically within **14-30 days** of the change (verify specific timeframe with DCRA)

## PART 6: COMPANY WIND-UP AND DISSOLUTION

### Types of Winding Up

**Voluntary Winding Up:**
- Initiated by shareholders
- Members' voluntary winding up (company solvent)
- Creditors' voluntary winding up (company insolvent)

**Involuntary/Compulsory Winding Up:**
- Court-ordered
- Usually due to inability to pay debts
- Non-compliance with statutory requirements

### Liquidation Process
- Appointment of liquidator
- Settlement of debts and liabilities
- Distribution of remaining assets
- Final accounting and reports
- Application for dissolution

### Removal from Register
Companies can be struck off for:
- Failure to file annual returns
- Failure to pay fees
- Cessation of operations
- Voluntary strike-off application

### Restoration of Companies
Struck-off companies can apply for restoration under certain conditions

## PART 7: EXTERNAL COMPANIES

### Definition
Companies incorporated outside Guyana but conducting business within Guyana

### Registration Requirement
External companies must register with DCRA if:
- Establishing a place of business in Guyana
- Conducting regular business operations
- Holding property in Guyana

### Registration Documents
- Certificate of incorporation from home jurisdiction
- Memorandum and Articles of Association
- List of directors and officers
- Appointment of local representative/agent
- Registered office address in Guyana

### Annual Compliance
- File annual returns
- Update changes to directors/structure
- Maintain registered office

### Cessation
Notify DCRA when ceasing operations in Guyana

## PART 8: PATENTS, TRADEMARKS, AND DESIGNS

### Patents
Registration of inventions and innovations

**Requirements:**
- Description of invention
- Claims defining protection scope
- Drawings/diagrams (if applicable)
- Declaration of inventorship

### Trademarks
Registration of brands, logos, and business identifiers

**Requirements:**
- Trademark representation (logo, wordmark)
- Goods/services classification
- Declaration of use or intent to use

### Designs
Registration of product designs and ornamental features

**Requirements:**
- Drawings/photographs of design
- Description of design features
- Application fees

### Application Process
1. Conduct search for existing registrations
2. Submit application with required documents
3. Examination by DCRA
4. Publication (for opposition period)
5. Registration and certificate issuance

### Validity and Renewal
- Patents, trademarks, and designs have specific validity periods
- Require periodic renewal to maintain protection
- Fees apply for renewals

---

## PART 9: BILLS OF SALE

### Definition
Legal document transferring ownership of movable property (goods, equipment, vehicles, etc.) from seller to buyer

### When to Register
Registration provides:
- Public notice of ownership transfer
- Protection against future claims
- Evidence of transaction

### Required Information
- Description of property
- Seller and buyer details
- Purchase price
- Date of sale
- Signatures and witnesses

---

## PART 10: DEBENTURES

### Definition
Debt instrument issued by a company, secured against company assets

### Registration
Debentures must be registered with DCRA to:
- Establish priority of security interest
- Provide public notice to creditors
- Protect debenture holder's rights

### Required Documents
- Debenture agreement
- Description of secured assets
- Terms and conditions
- Details of debenture holder

---

## PART 11: RECEIVERSHIP

### When Appointed
Receiver appointed when:
- Company defaults on secured debt
- Debenture holder enforces security
- Court orders receivership

### Notification to DCRA
- Appointment of receiver must be notified
- Updates on receivership status
- Final report upon completion

---

## PART 12: POST-REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS

### After Business Registration

**Immediate Next Steps:**

1. **Register with GRA**
   - Obtain Tax Identification Number (TIN)
   - Register for VAT (if applicable)
   - Register for PAYE (if hiring employees)
   - Timeline: 1 day at GRA main branch

2. **Register with NIS**
   - Employer registration (if hiring employees)
   - Obtain NIS employer number
   - Timeline: Approximately 1 week

3. **Obtain Business Licenses**
   - Municipal licenses (Georgetown City Council or relevant municipality)
   - Trade licenses from GRA Licence Revenue Office
   - Sector-specific licenses (if applicable)

4. **Open Business Bank Account**
   - Present Certificate of Business Registration
   - Company incorporation documents
   - Director identification
   - TIN

- Contractor registration
- Building permits

- Customs registration

### Professional Services

## PART 16: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### Business Name Registration
- ❌ Choosing a name too similar to existing businesses
- ❌ Not conducting proper name search
- ❌ Forgetting to renew registration

### Company Incorporation
- ❌ Incomplete Memorandum/Articles of Association
- ❌ Incorrect director/shareholder information
- ❌ Not maintaining registered office address
- ❌ Delaying incorporation documents preparation

### Annual Returns
- ❌ Missing filing deadlines
- ❌ Submitting incomplete financial statements
- ❌ Not updating director/shareholder changes
- ❌ Assuming no filing required if no business activity

### Beneficial Ownership
- ❌ Failing to file initial BOI declaration
- ❌ Not updating when ownership changes
- ❌ Incorrect identification of beneficial owners

## PART 20: Business Registration Workflow

### Complete Timeline

**Week 1:**
- Day 1: Conduct name search
- Day 2: Submit business name registration application
- Day 3-5: DCRA processes application
- **End of Week 1:** Receive Certificate of Business Registration

**Week 2:**
- Day 6: Visit GRA with DCRA certificate
- Day 6: Obtain TIN (same day at main branch)
- Day 7: Submit NIS employer registration (Form R1)

**Week 3:**
- Day 8-14: Wait for NIS approval (approximately 1 week)
- **End of Week 3:** Receive NIS employer registration

**Week 3-4:**
- Apply for trade/municipal licenses (with DCRA, GRA, and NIS documents)
- Open business bank account
- Commence operations

**Total Estimated Time:** 2-3 weeks from start to full compliance
