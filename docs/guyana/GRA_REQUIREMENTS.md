# Guyana Revenue Authority (GRA) Compliance Requirements

## Overview

The Guyana Revenue Authority (GRA) is the primary tax administration body in Guyana, responsible for administering and collecting all taxes, including Income Tax, Value Added Tax (VAT), Pay As You Earn (PAYE), Excise Tax, Property Tax, Capital Gains Tax, and Customs Duties.

**Official Website:** [www.gra.gov.gy](https://www.gra.gov.gy)
**eServices Portal:** [eservices.gra.gov.gy](https://eservices.gra.gov.gy)
**Contact Email:** filing@gra.gov.gy

---

## 1. Tax Identification Number (TIN)

### Purpose
A TIN is mandatory for all businesses and individuals conducting taxable activities in Guyana. It serves as a unique identifier for all tax-related transactions.

### Application Methods

#### Online Application
1. Create an account on the GRA eServices portal
2. Log in and check eligibility
3. Complete the TIN registration form
4. Upload required supporting documents
5. Submit application
6. Receive TIN certificate electronically

#### In-Person Application
1. Visit any GRA office
2. Complete TIN application form
3. Submit required documents
4. Receive TIN certificate (typically same day)

### Required Documents

**For Individuals:**
- Valid identification (passport, national ID, driver's license)
- Proof of address
- Birth certificate (if applicable)

**For Organizations:**
- Certificate of Business Registration from DCRA
- Company incorporation documents
- Identification of directors/shareholders
- Proof of business address
- Articles of Association and Memorandum (for companies)

### Processing Time
- Online: 1-3 business days
- In-person: Same day (at main GRA branch)

### First-Time Applicants
Email scanned copies of verification documents to: filing@gra.gov.gy

## 2. Value Added Tax (VAT)

### VAT Rate
Standard rate: **14%** (subject to periodic reviews)

### Registration Requirements

#### Mandatory Registration
Required when:
- Taxable activity equals or exceeds **G$15,000,000** at the end of twelve months
- Taxable activity exceeds the threshold in less than 12 months
- Taxable activity is expected to exceed the threshold during the next twelve months

#### Voluntary Registration
- Available for businesses below the G$15,000,000 threshold
- Business and business location must be easily identifiable
- Approval at GRA's discretion

### Registration Process
1. Apply online via eServices portal or visit GRA office
2. Submit business registration documents
3. Provide proof of business location
4. Receive VAT Certificate upon approval
5. **Important:** VAT Certificate must be displayed conspicuously at business location

### VAT Filing Requirements

#### Filing Frequency
**Monthly** - One calendar month constitutes a tax period

#### Deadline
- Returns due by the **21st of the following month**
- If the 21st falls on a holiday, the next business day applies
- Alternative specification: Within **15 official working days** after the end of the tax period

#### Submission Method
- Online via eServices: [eservices.gra.gov.gy](https://eservices.gra.gov.gy)
- Manual submission at GRA offices (discouraged)

#### Required Forms
- VAT Return form (available on GRA website and eServices)

### VAT Penalties

#### Civil Penalties for Late Filing
- **$1,000 per day** OR **10% of taxes to be paid** (whichever is greater)
- Maximum penalty: Cannot exceed the total taxes owed
- Applied for each day the return is late

#### Late Payment Penalties
- **2% per month** of the tax outstanding
- Applied for each month or part thereof that tax remains unpaid
- Compounds monthly

#### Criminal Penalties
**If knowingly or recklessly failing to submit VAT Return:**
- Fine up to **$15,000**
- **Imprisonment for 3 months** (possible)

**If granted extension but still fails to submit:**
- **$2,000 per day** fine
- **3 months imprisonment**

## 3. Pay As You Earn (PAYE)

### Overview
PAYE is income tax withheld from employees' wages and paid directly to the GRA by employers.

### Employer Responsibilities
- Deduct appropriate income tax from employees' salaries
- Remit combined employee deductions to GRA monthly
- Submit monthly and annual PAYE returns
- Maintain accurate payroll records

### Required Forms

#### Monthly Returns
- **Form 2:** Return to be made by Employers of Persons employed by them
- **Form 2 Summary Sheet and Spreadsheet**
- Due: **14th of each month**

#### Annual Returns
- **Form 5:** Return of Deductions of Tax by an Employer
- Due: **April 30** of the following year

### Filing Method
- Online submission via eServices (preferred): [eservices.gra.gov.gy](https://eservices.gra.gov.gy)
- Manual submission at GRA offices

### Income Tax Threshold Changes
Effective January 1, 2024 (Income Tax Amendment Act No. 2 of 2024):
- Updated personal allowances and deductions for employed and self-employed individuals
- Employers must implement changes in payroll systems

## 4. Corporate Tax

### Corporate Tax Rates
**Standard Rate:** 27-30% (verify current rate on GRA website)

### Filing Requirements

#### Annual Corporate Tax Return
- Due: **April 30** of the year following the year of income
- File with complete financial statements
- Include tax computation sheets
- Attach supporting schedules

#### Quarterly Estimated Tax Payments
Some businesses may be required to make quarterly estimated tax payments (confirm with GRA)

---

## 5. Income Tax (Individual)

### Filing Deadline
- Annual Individual Income Tax Return due: **April 30** of each year

### Required Documents
- Completed Individual Income Tax Return form
- Income statements (employment, self-employment, rental, etc.)
- Capital gains computation sheet (if applicable)
- Receipts for deductible expenses

### Personal Allowances (2024-2025)
Subject to annual updates - check GRA notices for current rates

---

## 6. Capital Gains Tax

### Tax Rate
**20%** of net chargeable gains

### Taxable Events
Disposal of capital assets including:
- Real property (land, buildings)
- Shares and securities
- Business assets

### Exemptions
- Gains treated as profit/income under Income Tax Act
- Transactions over **25 years** after asset acquisition
- Gains received within **12 months** after change of ownership (treated as chargeable income for Income Tax)
- Gains not exceeding **G$500,000**

### Filing Requirements
- Submit with Individual Income Tax Return
- Include computation sheet
- Due: **April 30** annually

---

## 7. Property Tax

### Overview
Payable by individuals on net property held at December 31 of each year

### Taxable Property
- Tangible property
- Intangible property
- Cash and receivables
- Other rights and assets

### Calculation
- Liabilities deducted from total property value
- Payable at progressive rates

### Filing
Check GRA website for current Property Tax return forms and rates

---

## 8. Withholding Tax

### Rates
**10%-15%** on payments to non-residents (and residents in specific cases)

### Application
- Payments for services
- Royalties
- Interest
- Dividends
- Management fees

### Filing Requirements
- **Monthly returns** due on or before the **14th of each month**
- File online via eServices

## 10. Trade & Miscellaneous Licenses

### Licensing Authority
Licence Revenue Office, Guyana Revenue Authority

### Required Documents
- Approved building permit
- Fire safety certificate
- Sanitary certificates
- TIN
- Business registration certificate

### Locations
- Smyth and Princes Streets, Georgetown
- Railway Embankment (Camp & Lamaha Streets)

### Fees
Vary by business type and location - contact GRA for fee schedule

### Provisional Licenses
Available for small businesses (grocery shops, variety stores, snackettes) to start operations while obtaining required permits

## 11. Certificate of Compliance

### Purpose
Proof that all outstanding tax returns are filed and all taxes are paid or arrangements made

### When Required
- Sale, transfer, gift, or lease of property (per Deeds Registry Act and Land Registry Act)
- Government contracts and tenders (per Procurement Act)
- Business transactions requiring tax clearance

### Eligibility Criteria
1. Must have a valid TIN
2. Must have submitted returns for the **past 3 years** (including preceding year of income)
3. All taxes and duties must be paid, or satisfactory payment arrangements made

### Taxes Included in Compliance Check
- Customs Duties
- Income Tax
- PAYE
- Excise Tax
- VAT
- Property Tax

## 18. Common Compliance Pitfalls

### Avoid These Mistakes:
1. **Late VAT Filing** - Heavy penalties apply; file by 21st monthly
2. **Incorrect VAT Calculations** - Ensure proper understanding of VAT rules
3. **Missing PAYE Deadlines** - Can result in employer penalties
4. **Not Displaying VAT Certificate** - Required by law at business location
5. **Incomplete Documentation** - Always attach required supporting documents
6. **Ignoring Notices** - Respond promptly to all GRA correspondence
7. **Misclassifying Business Type** - Affects tax rates and requirements
