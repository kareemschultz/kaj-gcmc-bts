# National Insurance Scheme (NIS) Guyana - Compliance Requirements

## Overview

The National Insurance Scheme (NIS) of Guyana is a social security system that provides benefits to contributors for sickness, maternity, invalidity, retirement, employment injury, and survivors' benefits. All employers, employees, and self-employed persons are required to contribute to the scheme.

**Official Website:** [www.nis.org.gy](https://www.nis.org.gy)
**Electronic Schedule Submission:** [esched.nis.org.gy](https://esched.nis.org.gy)

---

## 1. Employer Registration

### When to Register
- **Immediately** upon hiring your first employee
- **Before** commencing business operations with employees
- Required for all employers regardless of business size

### Registration Form
**Form R1:** Application for Registration as an Employer

### Where to Obtain Forms
- Download from official NIS website: [www.nis.org.gy](https://www.nis.org.gy)
- Visit NIS offices in person
- Available in PDF format for printing

### Required Information for Registration
- Business name and registration number
- Business address and contact information
- Type of business/industry
- Number of employees
- Expected start date of operations
- TIN (Tax Identification Number)
- Owner/Director information

### Processing Time
Approximately **1 week** for NIS employer registration approval

### Registration Office Hours
Check NIS website for current office hours and locations

---

## 2. Employee Registration

### Employer Responsibilities
- Register all employees with NIS upon hiring
- Obtain National Insurance (NI) number for each employee
- Maintain accurate records of employee NI numbers

### Employee NI Number
- Employees should provide their existing NI number if already registered
- For first-time workers, employers assist with registration
- NI number is unique and permanent for each individual

## 3. Contribution Rates (2024)

### Employed Persons

#### Total Contribution Rate: **14%** of gross salary

**Breakdown:**
- **Employee Contribution:** 5.6% (deducted from employee's pay)
- **Employer Contribution:** 8.4% (paid by employer on behalf of employee)

**Example Calculation:**
```
Gross Monthly Salary: $100,000
Employee Contribution (5.6%): $5,600 (deducted from salary)
Employer Contribution (8.4%): $8,400 (paid by employer)
Total NIS Contribution: $14,000
Employee Net Deduction: $5,600
```

### Self-Employed Persons

**Contribution Rate:** 12.5% of declared income

**Note:** Self-employed individuals are responsible for calculating and remitting their own contributions based on declared business income.

## 4. Contribution Ceilings (2024)

### Monthly Ceiling
**$280,000** per month maximum insurable earnings

### Weekly Ceiling
**$64,615** per week maximum insurable earnings

### Annual Ceiling
**$3,360,000** per year maximum insurable earnings

### What This Means
- Contributions are calculated only up to the ceiling amount
- Earnings above the ceiling are not subject to NIS contributions
- Ceilings are reviewed periodically and subject to change

## 5. Maximum Contribution Amounts

### Monthly Maximum Contributions

**Employee Contribution (5.6% of $280,000):**
- **$15,680** per month maximum

**Employer Contribution (8.4% of $280,000):**
- **$23,520** per month maximum

**Total Combined (14% of $280,000):**
- **$39,200** per month maximum

### Weekly Maximum Contributions

**Employee Contribution (5.6% of $64,615):**
- **$3,618** per week maximum

**Employer Contribution (8.4% of $64,615):**
- **$5,427** per week maximum

**Total Combined:**
- **$9,045** per week maximum

## 6. Contribution Schedules

### Filing Frequency

**Monthly:**
- For employees paid on a monthly basis
- Most common schedule type

**Weekly:**
- For employees paid on a weekly basis
- Common in certain industries (construction, retail, etc.)

### Required Forms

**Monthly Contributions:**
- **Monthly Contribution Schedule Form (CS3)**
- Employee names, NI numbers, wages, contributions

**Weekly Contributions:**
- **Weekly Contribution Schedule**
- Similar format to monthly but submitted weekly

### Where to Obtain Forms
- Download from [www.nis.org.gy](https://www.nis.org.gy)
- Electronic templates available at [esched.nis.org.gy](https://esched.nis.org.gy)
- Available at NIS offices

## 7. Electronic Schedule Submission

### Online Portal
**URL:** [esched.nis.org.gy](https://esched.nis.org.gy)

### System Requirements
- Internet connection
- Web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge recommended)
- Excel or compatible spreadsheet software

### How It Works

1. **Create Account**
   - Register on the eSchedule portal
   - Provide employer registration details
   - Verify email and account

2. **Download Fresh Schedule Template**
   - **Important:** Download updated template each filing period
   - Templates include current insurable earnings ceilings
   - Ensures compliance with latest contribution rates

3. **Complete Schedule**
   - Enter employee details (Name, NI Number, Gross Wages)
   - System auto-calculates contributions
   - Review summary sheet

4. **Upload Schedule**
   - Create summary sheet details on portal
   - Attach completed schedule file
   - Submit electronically

5. **Confirmation**
   - Receive submission confirmation
   - Print/save receipt for records

### Benefits of Electronic Submission
- Faster processing
- Automatic calculations reduce errors
- Immediate confirmation of receipt
- Reduced paper usage
- Accessible from anywhere

### Who Can Use It
- **Monthly paid employees:** Full system available
- **Weekly paid employees:** System available (check current status)

## 8. Contribution Payment

### Payment Methods

**Bank Transfer:**
- Preferred method for larger employers
- Transfer to designated NIS bank account
- Include employer registration number as reference

**In-Person Payment:**
- At NIS offices nationwide
- Cash or certified checks accepted
- Bring contribution schedule and payment

**Online Payment:**
- Check eSchedule portal for integrated payment options

### Payment Deadlines

**Important:** Specific deadlines should be verified with NIS directly, but generally:

**Monthly Schedules:**
- Due within specified days after month end
- Common deadline: 14th-15th of following month (verify)

**Annual NIS Returns:**
- Due: **January 31** of the following year
- Reconciliation of all contributions for the year

### Late Payment Consequences
- Penalties may apply
- Interest charges on overdue amounts
- Potential suspension of employer account
- Employees may be unable to claim benefits

---

## 9. Annual NIS Returns

### Filing Requirement
Employers must submit **annual NIS returns** detailing all contributions made for each employee during the calendar year.

### Deadline
**January 31** of the following year

**Example:** Annual return for 2024 is due by January 31, 2025

### What to Include
- Summary of all monthly/weekly contributions
- Employee-by-employee breakdown
- Total contributions paid vs. due
- Reconciliation of any discrepancies

### Importance
- Verifies employee contribution history
- Ensures employees can claim benefits when needed
- Required for NIS compliance status

## 10. Employer Responsibilities Summary

### Registration
- [ ] Register as employer using Form R1
- [ ] Maintain current employer registration
- [ ] Update NIS of any business changes (address, ownership, etc.)

### Employee Management
- [ ] Register all new employees immediately
- [ ] Obtain and verify NI numbers for all staff
- [ ] Maintain accurate employee records

### Contributions
- [ ] Calculate contributions correctly (5.6% + 8.4%)
- [ ] Deduct employee portion from wages
- [ ] Pay combined contribution to NIS by deadline
- [ ] Never deduct employee contribution without remitting to NIS

### Reporting
- [ ] Submit monthly or weekly contribution schedules on time
- [ ] Use correct and updated forms/templates
- [ ] Submit annual NIS return by January 31
- [ ] Respond promptly to NIS inquiries or audits

### Record Keeping
- [ ] Maintain payroll records for minimum 7 years
- [ ] Keep copies of all contribution schedules submitted
- [ ] Retain proof of payments
- [ ] Document employee NI numbers

## 11. Employee Rights and Benefits

### Benefits Covered by NIS

**Short-Term Benefits:**
- Sickness benefit
- Maternity benefit
- Employment injury benefit

**Long-Term Benefits:**
- Invalidity pension
- Retirement pension (age pension)
- Survivors' pension (for dependents)

### Eligibility
Benefits are based on contributions made. Employers must ensure:
- Timely and accurate contribution submissions
- Employees are properly registered
- Records are maintained for benefit claims

### Employee Inquiries
Direct employees to:
- NIS website for benefit information
- NIS offices for benefit claims
- Online portals for contribution history

## 13. Self-Employed Persons

### Registration Requirement
All self-employed persons with business income must register with NIS.

### Registration Form
**NIS Self-Employment Registration Form**

### Contribution Rate
**12.5%** of declared income

### How to Calculate
```
Annual Business Income: $1,000,000
NIS Contribution (12.5%): $125,000
```

### Payment Schedule
- Quarterly or annually (verify current requirements with NIS)
- Based on declared business income
- Subject to minimum and maximum contribution rules

### Benefits
Self-employed contributors are eligible for:
- Retirement pension
- Invalidity pension
- Survivors' pension
- Employment injury benefit (in certain cases)

## 14. Compliance and Audits

### NIS Inspections
NIS may conduct:
- Routine compliance audits
- Random inspections of employer records
- Investigations based on complaints or discrepancies

### What Inspectors Check
- Accurate calculation of contributions
- Timely submission of schedules
- Proper employee registration
- Correct application of insurable earnings ceiling
- Complete and accurate payroll records

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Fines for late or incorrect filings
- Penalties for non-payment
- Interest on overdue contributions
- Legal action for persistent non-compliance
- Possible business license implications

## 15. Updates and Changes to NIS

### Annual Updates
**Important:** NIS contribution ceilings, rates, and requirements may change annually.

**How to Stay Informed:**
- Visit [www.nis.org.gy](https://www.nis.org.gy) regularly
- Subscribe to NIS email updates
- Check notices published in newspapers
- Download fresh electronic schedules (which reflect current ceilings)

### 2024-2025 Changes
Always verify the current year's:
- Contribution rates (currently 5.6% + 8.4%)
- Insurable earnings ceilings (currently $280,000/month)
- Maximum contributions
- Filing deadlines

## 16. Integration with GRA Requirements

### Coordinated Compliance
NIS and GRA compliance are interconnected:

**For Business Permits:**
- Require both GRA compliance certificate and NIS compliance

**For Tenders and Contracts:**
- Must show NIS compliance status
- Must show GRA compliance certificate

**Registration Sequence:**
1. Register business with DCRA (Deeds and Commercial Registries Authority)
2. Register with GRA for Tax Identification Number (TIN)
3. Register with NIS as employer

**Documentation Needed:**
- Certificate of Business Registration (from DCRA)
- TIN (from GRA)
- NIS card (for self-employed individuals)

## 17. Common Errors to Avoid

### Registration Errors
- ❌ Delaying employer registration until after hiring
- ❌ Not registering employees upon hiring
- ❌ Using incorrect or outdated NI numbers

### Calculation Errors
- ❌ Applying contribution rates to earnings above ceiling
- ❌ Incorrect percentage calculations (using wrong rates)
- ❌ Forgetting to update rates when they change

### Filing Errors
- ❌ Using outdated contribution schedule templates
- ❌ Missing submission deadlines
- ❌ Incomplete employee information
- ❌ Mathematical errors in totals

### Payment Errors
- ❌ Deducting employee contribution but not remitting to NIS
- ❌ Late payment without communication with NIS
- ❌ Paying incorrect amounts

## 19. NIS Compliance Checklist

### One-Time Setup
- [ ] Register as employer (Form R1)
- [ ] Receive employer registration number
- [ ] Create eSchedule portal account
- [ ] Set up contribution payment method

### Monthly Tasks
- [ ] Calculate employee contributions (5.6% of gross wages up to ceiling)
- [ ] Calculate employer contributions (8.4% of gross wages up to ceiling)
- [ ] Deduct employee portion from wages

- [ ] Submit schedule by deadline
- [ ] Pay total contributions to NIS

### New Hires
- [ ] Register new employee immediately
- [ ] Obtain/verify NI number
- [ ] Add to contribution schedule starting first pay period

### Annually
- [ ] Submit annual NIS return by January 31
- [ ] Reconcile all contributions for the year
- [ ] Verify employee records are accurate

### Ongoing
- [ ] Download fresh electronic schedule template each filing period
- [ ] Keep payroll and NIS records for 7+ years
- [ ] Update NIS of business changes (address, ownership, etc.)
- [ ] Monitor NIS website for rate/ceiling changes
